Retail Market Analysis

Syria's retail sector is in early-stage recovery. The pre-2011 nascent modern retail scene was largely destroyed or frozen during 14 years of conflict. Current state: predominantly traditional retail (souks, independent shops), with modern retail concentrated in Damascus. The kids fashion category is almost entirely served by informal/parallel imports and unbranded local manufacturers — a structural gap Okaidi is uniquely positioned to fill.

SWOT Analysis

STRENGTHS

  • First-mover — zero formal kids fashion competitors
  • Demographic tailwind — 40% under 15
  • NDS owns Galleria Hijaz — no landlord risk
  • Turkey proximity — Okaidi already operates there
  • Deep Syria roots — Samha family connections
  • Multi-child households → larger basket
  • Okaidi brand recognition among returning diaspora
  • Small store format (100–120 sqm) lowers CAPEX risk

WEAKNESSES

  • Cash economy — limited digital payments
  • Small upper-middle class (~5%) at current incomes
  • Kids retail unfamiliar with formal brand experience
  • Profit repatriation challenges (improving)
  • Energy infrastructure — backup power required
  • Initial inventory and COGS structure TBD
  • Staff training to international kids retail standards

OPPORTUNITIES

  • 7–9M children under 15 with no formal retail
  • GDP growth ~10% 2026
  • Sanctions lifted — barriers removed
  • Returning diaspora expanding upper-middle class
  • Brand nostalgia: "the brands are coming back"
  • Visa deal → digital payments coming
  • Eid ×2 + wedding + back-to-school peaks
  • 4 cities mapped for expansion

THREATS

  • SYP currency volatility (60% band in 2025)
  • Political instability — northeast tensions
  • Parallel imports from Turkey
  • Consumer purchasing power constraints
  • Customs/import bureaucracy
  • Energy disruptions
  • Competitor entry (Zara Kids/H&M may follow)
  • Property rights uncertainty
